The Story Behind the Flavors

Goan cuisine is a beautiful fusion born from centuries of cultural exchange. When the Portuguese arrived in 1510, they brought new ingredients, cooking techniques, and culinary traditions that blended harmoniously with local Konkani flavors.

Over time, many traditional recipes have been lost or altered. Family secrets passed down through generations are disappearing as urbanization changes how we cook and eat. Our mission is to document and preserve these culinary treasures before they vanish forever.

Traditional Bebinca

A rich, layered Goan dessert made with coconut milk, eggs, and palm sugar. This Portuguese-influenced sweet requires patience but rewards with incredible flavor.

"Bebinca is the queen of Goan desserts, traditionally served during Christmas. Each layer represents patience and love."

3 hours
8 servings
Hard

Key Ingredients:

  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 10 egg yolks
  • 1 cup palm sugar (jaggery)
  • 1/2 cup plain flour
  • 1/4 cup ghee
  • 1 tsp nutmeg powder
